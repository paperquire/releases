# Climate Change (ESRS E1)

## Transition Plan

GreenTech has committed to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2040, with the following interim targets:

| Target | Baseline (2020) | Current (2025) | 2030 Target | 2040 Target |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Scope 1 emissions | 42,000 tCO₂e | 28,500 tCO₂e | 21,000 tCO₂e (−50%) | Near zero |
| Scope 2 emissions (market-based) | 68,000 tCO₂e | 31,200 tCO₂e | 13,600 tCO₂e (−80%) | Zero |
| Scope 3 emissions | 285,000 tCO₂e | 256,000 tCO₂e | 199,500 tCO₂e (−30%) | −70% |

### Key Decarbonization Initiatives

1. **Renewable energy procurement** — 72% of electricity from renewable PPAs (target: 100% by 2028)
2. **Manufacturing electrification** — Replacement of natural gas furnaces with electric induction (3 of 8 lines converted)
3. **Fleet electrification** — 45% of company vehicles are electric (target: 100% by 2030)
4. **Supplier engagement** — Top 50 suppliers (covering 78% of Scope 3) enrolled in carbon reduction program

# Workers in the Value Chain (ESRS S2)

## Due Diligence Process

GreenTech conducts human rights due diligence in accordance with the UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights and the forthcoming EU Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive (CSDDD):

1. **Risk mapping** — Annual assessment of human rights risks across the value chain, covering 380 Tier 1 suppliers and 120 Tier 2 suppliers
2. **Audits** — On-site audits of high-risk suppliers (28 audits conducted in 2025)
3. **Remediation** — Corrective action plans for identified issues (12 active plans)
4. **Grievance mechanism** — Whistleblower hotline and supplier grievance channel available in 8 languages
